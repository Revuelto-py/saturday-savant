How Savant Forecast Works

Savant Forecast is a logistic-regression model trained on ten seasons of FBS games (2016–2025). For each matchup it weighs a rolling team-strength rating — updated after every game by result, margin, and venue — together with signals that are known before a season starts: last season's Savant Rating and SP+, how much production each roster returns, four years of recruiting class strength, and scoring form so far this season. The output is a probability, not a pick.

When it says 65%, it means 65%. On the held-out 2025 test season, stated probabilities matched reality in every bucket:

Measured accuracy — including the benchmark that beats us. On the 2025 season (808 games, never used in training):

PredictorStraight-up accuracy
Always pick the home team59.5%
Pick the higher prior-season SP+65.4%
Team-strength rating alone70.9%
Savant Forecast71.8%
Vegas closing line73.5%

The Vegas closing line is the strongest public predictor in existence, and it beats this model by about a point and a half — as it beats essentially every published model. We show that number anyway; a forecast you can't compare against the market is a forecast you can't trust.

What it never uses. Nothing the model sees would be unknowable before kickoff. End-of-season ratings from the season being predicted are excluded (they contain the very results being forecast), and AP poll positions are excluded entirely. When a forecast is made, it uses only games already played and preseason information.

Read early-season forecasts loosely. In September the model leans mostly on preseason priors — roster returns, recruiting, last year's ratings — so weeks 1–3 carry wider uncertainty than November games. Its measured 2025 accuracy was 72% in weeks 1–3, 69% in its weakest stretch (weeks 4–8, while in-season data is still thin), and 74% from week 9 on. And a probability is never a lock: a 75% favorite loses one time in four.
